EARLY HISTORY


EARLY HISTORY OF THE NIGERIAN BAR ASSOCIATION, OTA BRANCH

Introduction The preamble date_range

Upon the creation of Ota Judicial Division which was carved out from Ilaro Judicial Division in 1989, Ota Judicial Division took off from the premises that house the present Ota Magistrates Courts. The only notable practicing lawyers on ground at Ota prior to the creation of Ota Judicial Division that time are Chief Olayinka Shotayo and His Royal Highness, Oba Olufemi Ojugbele. It should be noted that these two founding fathers are indigenes and illustrious sons of the soil. The two dynamic fathers and leaders hosted all the first set of lawyers that came to sojourn and practice at Ota Judicial Division and these lawyers include Mr. Aina, Mr. Olatunde Opaleye, Honourable Justice S. M. Owodunnin, Mr. F. O. Kehinde, Mr. Martins Onoja, Chief Oluseun Takintayo Akinbiyi (SAN), Mr. J. T. Ogunniyi (FICMC), Mr. Chris U. Anyanwu, Mr.Kingsley Oba Bazuaye, Mr. George Okey Chukwudobe, Mr. Nwosu, Mr. Akeem Alagbe, Mr. C. A. Nwakpo, Honourable Justice Laja Rafiu Durojaiye, Mr. G.O.D Okeke, Mr. David Taiwo Ojo, Elder S. A. Ameh etc.

The first set of judges that hosted Ota Judicial Division are Late Honourable Jusitice Odubiyi and Honourable Justice Gabsiu Bakre (Rtd), while the Magistrates are Chief Magistrate Adesanya (Rtd), Chief Magistrate Akindele (Rtd) and Honourable Justice Odutan and the current Chief Judge of Ogun State Honourable Justice Mosunmola Dipeolu (FICMC).

Early Formation The first meeting date_range

In a bid to bring the few lawyers around Ota Judicial Division together, The first meeting of lawyers then practicing and based in the Ota Judicial Division of the High Court of Ogun State was held on the 28th of June, 1999 in High Court No. 1 (within the old town). The meeting was summoned by Chief Olayinka Sotayo who was by far the most senior at the time. It was explained at the meeting that several unsuccessful attempts were made before to gather lawyers based in the area together. Hon. (Mr.) Justice Olatunde Odubiyi (now late) and Hon. (Mr.) Justice Gabsiu Bakre who were the first judges to sit in Ota, gave inspiration to the formation of an Ota Branch of Nigeria Bar Association.

At the meeting after many of those present had spoken glowingly about the need to have an Ota Branch of the Nigeria Bar Association, and as it was clear that the number of lawyers based in the area exceeded the ten required by the Constitution of the Nigeria Bar Association at the time, Mr. David Taiwo Ojo moved the motion for the formation of the Branch while Mr. Francis Oluwaleke Kehinde supported the motion. All eleven (11) lawyers present voted in support. Ota Branch of the Nigeria Bar Association was thus formed de facto. Those present at the meeting (in the order in which their names were recorded) were:

  1. Chief Olayinka Sotayo
  2. Mr. Joseph Taiwo Ogunniyi
  3. Mr. Francis Oluwaleke Kehinde
  4. Mr. George Okey Chukwudobe
  5. Mr. Sikiru Mohamed Owodunni (now Hon. Mr. Justice S.M. Owodunni)
  6. Mr. David Taiwo Ojo
  7. Mr. Kingsley Oba Bazuaye
  8. Mr. Oluseun Takintayo Akinbiyi
  9. Mr. Olatunde Opaleye
  10. Mr. Martins Onoja
  11. Mr. Chris Anyawu

The next meeting of the Branch was held on 12th July, 1991 when the first set of officers of the Branch were elected as follows:

  1. Chief Olayinka Shotayo - Chairman
  2. Chief (now Oba) Olufemi Ojugbele - Vice Chairman
  3. Mr. Oluseun T. Akinbiyi - Secretary
  4. Mr. (now Hon. Justice) S.M. Owodunni - Treasurer
  5. Mr. Martins Onoja - Publicity and Social Secretary
  6. Mr. (now Prof.) C.U. Anyanwu - Financial Secretary
  7. Mr. J.T. Ogunniyi - Auditor
  8. Mr. G.O. Chukwudobe - Assistant Secretary

Another major decision made on 12th July, 1991 was that the meetings of the Branch should be held fortnightly until it was found necessary to change the period.

Registration The serious challenges date_range

In those years no extraneous factors were attached to the formal establishment (or registration) of a branch of the Nigeria Bar Association other than that there should be a judicial division of a Court and that the number of lawyers practicing in the domain of the intended branch should not be less than 10(ten). It was therefore presumed that the registration, formal establishment or de jure formation of Ota Branch was going to be a mere formality. But an application for the registration was not submitted immediately after the Branch was formed until August, 1992 during the Annual General Conference of that year held in Port Harcourt. Tragically, the first Chairman of the Branch died and unfortunately the Nigerian Bar Association became comatose from the Conference as a result of an irreconcilable conflict in a struggle for the leadership of the Association. The Association was revived only in 1998 and hence the application of the Branch for registration could not have been considered as there was no National Executive Committee in place.

Once the Bar Association was revived the issue of the registration of Ota Branch became a recurrent topic at various meetings of the National Executive Committee of the NBA and even at its Annual General Conference. But by then, the issue of the registration of branches had become very highly politicized in view of the importance it had attained for the purpose of election of national officers (especially President and General Secretary) of the Association. At the local level some of the older Branches in Ogun State appeared opposed to the registration or emergence of Ota Branch.

Notwithstanding the challenges the Branch faced it was acknowledged and accorded recognition everywhere especially from 1994. Very remarkably, the Branch was invited to a meeting of the caretakers of the Nigerian Bar Association on Friday, 19th of May, 1995 at the Nigerian Law School. The Branch was also invited to the 1995 Annual Conference of the Nigerian Bar Association. At the State level the Branch was invited to several functions of the Judiciary including the Special Service for the opening of the 1995/96 Legal Year. The Branch was also invited to submit a report to the panel set up on the review of Court fees by Ogun State Judiciary in 1995. The Chief Registrar of the High Court of Ogun State (at the time) invited the Branch to submit a list containing the names and particulars of the members of the Executive of the Branch for 1994/95 Legal Year. The Magistrates’ Association of Nigeria (Ogun State Branch) notified the Branch of some of its activities by correspondence.

At the local level the Branch received a correspondence as a Branch from a reputable insurance company within the community. In September, 1995 the Branch was invited into the Police Community Relations Committee of the Nigeria Police Divisional Headquarters, Sango-Ota.

As for itself the Branch marched forward. It organized its first Special Public Lecture which took place on Friday, 2nd December, 1994 at the Local Government Council Community Hall. The Lecture was titled “Visibility of the End of Law in Contemporary Nigeria: Is Law Still Relevant?” The Lecture was delivered by a renowned Professor of Jurisprudence, Professor I.A. Agbede. It was attended by a sizeable audience and was widely reported in both the print and broadcast media especially on NTA Network News. The first Annual Dinner was held in April, 1995 and was utilized as a formal sent-forth party for the Hon. Justices G.A. Bakre and E.O. Olatoye who were transferred from the Ota Judicial Division around that period. Another Public Lecture was held on the 5th of July, 1996 in which one of the best lawyers in the profession – Mr. Kehinde Sofola, SAN. delivered a lecture on “The Legal Profession” also at the Local Government Council Community Hall, Ota. In November, 1999 the Branch introduced the Quarterly Bar-Bench Forum and held the maiden event on 10th November. It is a program which aims at a closer and better relationship between the Bench and members of the Bar who practice in Ota Judicial Division of the High Court of Ogun State; and generally to enhance the quality of practice of the legal profession. At each event current issues relating to practice and the Courts were discussed in the first session and in the second session a lecture is given by a member of the Bench or the Bar for discussion. It was always beautiful and well attended. Generally although members were few but meetings were held regularly and very important decisions were taken as to matters relating to the welfare of members, work and efficiency of the courts and issues of ethical conduct and discipline among members. Things were largely alright.

Approval Success at last date_range

After a long persistence and years of virility, Ota Branch was eventually registered and approved on Thursday the 25th of June, 2001 in Zaria by the National Executive Committee of the NBA. From then on the Branch has grown from strength to strength with a lot of enviable achievements through very purposeful and hard work of leaders of the Branch and unrelenting co-operation of members.
