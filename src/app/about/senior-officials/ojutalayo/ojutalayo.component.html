OLUSOLA O. OJUTALAYO, ESQ Principal Partner @ Sola Ojutalayo & Co.

Citation

Olusola O. OJUTALAYO, Esq. S.A.N. hails from Ile-Ife, Osun State but is well integrated into Ogun State which he regards as a second home having spent all his professional years in the State.

He had his secondary education at the renowned Christ’s School, Ado-Ekiti, Ekiti State, where he obtained the West African School Certificate in 1976 and proceeded to the University of Ife, Ile-Ife (now Obafemi Awolowo University) where he bagged his Bachelor of Laws Degree (LL.B) Hons. in the year 1981.

He was admitted to the Nigerian Law School, Victoria Island, Lagos and was called to the Nigerian Bar in the year 1982.

O. O. Ojutalayo Esq. S.A.N. began his legal career at the Ministry of Justice, Ogun State as a Youth Corps Member between 1982 - 1983. By reason of excellent performance and diligence, he was retained as a State Counsel by that Ministry upon his conclusion of national service. He was at the said Ministry as State Counsel from 1983 -1985.

On the invitation of the Principal Counsel coupled with his quest for a more robust and challenging law practice, O. O. Ojutalayo Esq joined the law office of Messrs, Coker, Fadayiro & Associates in Abeokuta as an Associate from 1985 to 1988.

He rose to the position of Senior Counsel in the then law firm of Chief Toye Coker SAN & Co. and diligently handled many briefs in that office till 1990. He became the Head of Chambers of Chief Toye Coker & Co. in 1991. As the Head of Chambers, O. O. Ojutalayo efficiently managed the office and professionally conducted his cases in all tiers of Court in Nigeria with acclaimed dexterity till 1998 when he felt it was time for him to move on.

He went on to establish his own law firm of Messrs Sola Ojutalayo & Co. in 1998 and that office has continued to wax strong till date.

Olusola O. Ojutalayo is a seasoned lawyer with an array of experience spanning litigation, arbitration, mediation, commercial/ corporate practice. He has handled myriad of cases in many aspects of the law, such as land, chieftaincy, trademarks, patent and designs, contract and commercial transactions, company proceedings, probate, election petition, constitutional law, trusteeship and inheritance as well as general criminal and civil litigations.

O. O. Ojutalayo, S.A.N. has shown prowess and commitment to research as can be gleaned from his numerous presentations and publications, which include but not limited to:

  • “Locus standi – Its meaning and its resolution in our Courts”: Contribution to Landmark cases and Essays in Honour of Kehinde Sofola, S.A.N., C.O.N.”
  • “Conflicts and Disharmony between the Three Arms of Government in Nigeria; ‘A Recipe for Chaos and Stunted Development.’ Contribution to a Compendium of Essays in Honour of A Quintessential Jurist: The Honourable Justice Olatokunbo Olopade, CON, KJW, The First Female Judge of Ogun State.”
  • “Effective Justice Delivery –A Legal Practitioner’s Perspective- A paper delivered as a resource person at the Ogun State Judges’ Conference, Abeokuta.”
  • “Judiciary in the Era of Elections in Nigeria – Being a paper delivered as a resource person at the Magistrates’ conference, Abeokuta, Ogun State.”
  • “Formation and Determination of contracts of Employment” – Being a paper delivered as a resource person at a workshop organized by the Otta Bar on Essentials of Industrial Labour Relations in a Growing economy.
  • “Democracy and the Rule of Law in emergent Constitutional Nigerian State.”
  • “Fight Against Corruption – Duty of All” - A paper delivered as a resource person at the Annual Gala/Award Nite, of the Nigerian Bar Association, Otta Branch.”
  • “Role of the Traditional Rulers in sustaining Democracy in a Contemporary Nigeria” – Published in the Voice Magazine, April, 2017.”
  • “Prevention of Economic Financial Crime; the Social Legal and Situational Strategies”; a paper presented at the Conference of Officium Legis, at Obasanjo Presidential Library, Abeokuta, Ogun State.

Aside his core legal practice, the Learned Silk has also held several positions both within and outside the NBA:

  • He was a former Chairman, Nigerian Bar Association, Ota Branch.
  • Former Assistant Secretary, Nigerian Bar Association, Abeokuta Branch.
  • Former Member of the National Executive Committee of the Nigerian Bar Association.
  • Member of the Disciplinary Committee of the NBA constituted for Ogun State.
  • Currently, member of the Law Reporting Committee of Ogun State Judiciary.
  • Member, Planning Committee of the Bar & Bench Forum, Ogun State.
  • Member, International Bar Association.
  • ne time Chairman, Local Government Election Tribunal, Ogun State in 2012.
  • Chairman Zone XI Security Stakeholders Forum (NPF).

O. O. Ojutalayo Esq. was appointed Notary Public by the Honourable, the Chief Justice of Nigeria in 2011 and by dint of hardwork and divine grace he was elevated and conferred with the prestigious rank of Senior Advocate of Nigeria in 2016.

In the trajectory of his rise to the peak of his career O. O. Ojutalayo Esq bagged numerous social, religious and professional awards from diverse bodies and institutions among which is that of “Role Model at the Bar” from the NBA, Ogun State.

He has attended several professional, social, National and International conferences where he delivered lectures and papers on both law and other issues affecting the legal profession and our dear country, Nigeria.

His hobbies include reading and music.

He is happily married with children.
